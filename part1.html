Modeling notes & assumptions

Rating = P(reach mahjong), solo model. We hold your 13 tiles and draw K tiles from the unseen wall (no opponents, no calling/exposures). The rating is the fraction of Monte-Carlo trials in which some 2026 card hand becomes fully coverable from hand + draws, with jokers substituting only in groups of 3+ (never pairs/singles, never soap-as-zero singles). K is the "draws available" knob — a full wall is ~24–25 picks; ~16 reflects a realistic competitive game.

Distance = minimum tiles still needed to complete the closest concrete target (joker-aware). Reach-mass = Σ exp(−0.8·d) over all card lines — a single deterministic strength surrogate that correlates with the Monte-Carlo probability at r=0.89 (R²≈0.80) and carries the strongest independent signal of any metric (partial r=0.75 controlling for distance & jokers). It is the fast inner-loop scorer the Charleston optimizer uses, so passes can be ranked without a full MC each.

Charleston optimizer. Every distinct 3-tile pass is scored by expected post-pass reach-mass, averaging over sampled tiles you receive (modeled as random from the unseen pool — a later version can weight by opponents' likely discards). This beats the v1 "drop your 3 worst by distance" heuristic: e.g. on a hand building a Bam run, the heuristic would shed a run tile, while the optimizer keeps the structure and passes the isolated tiles instead.

Card source. All 9 sections / 72 lines of the 2026 card are encoded (≈1,083 concrete targets vs. NMJL's stated 1,077). A handful of multi-suit lines have an inferred suit-colour partition (marked ~role-inferred in engine.js); correct against your physical card for exact analysis.

Known approximations. The optimizer fixes the candidate-hand set from your dealt hand and scores with reach-mass rather than a full MC per pass (for speed); received tiles are uniform-random from the unseen pool. These are the next things to refine.
